Esteam

Esteam is an application to promote medication adherence among patients suffering from depression. It takes a community building approach based on goal setting, priming, storytelling, and behavioral change.

The Problem

Chronic illness in the U.S. is becoming more prevalent each year as people live longer. Many of these diseases are treated through pharmacotherapy. The World Health Organization expects 157 million Americans to have at least one chronic disease with required medication by 2020. However, medication adherence is a major issue among chronic disease patients. Only about 50% of people who are prescribed medicine take the medication as prescribed [1]. Depression is one of the most common diseases worldwide for which there is a need of better medication adherence. Adherence for people with depression is very low. While adherence drops to 50% in six months for patients with any disease, as mentioned above, adherence in patients with depression drops to 50% in only three months.

Final Prototype - Onboarding

We designed our onboarding process to quick and hassle-free yet capturing information such as instrumental values (based on Rokeach Value Survey [6]) and a purpose [7] for priming the user to develop a positive motivation towards medication. Groups of eight are formed based on these, and their state of medication adherence. This state is determined by the ‘State of Change’ section to make the group composition to be a heterogenous mix of people at various stages of change, in alignment with the Transtheoretical Model [8].

Final Prototype - Home

For brevity of this page, I will be listing key functionality of the application. For a comprehensive discussion, please refer to the document here.
Home page is the central location that ties in various aspects of the application. It primarily contains,

  • Instrumental values and purpose image are persistently shown thereby priming the user to elicit an implicit association with their values, purpose, and medication
  • A weekly stats chart displaying their adherence, and clicking on which opens a detailed stats view
  • A daily checklist of medicines to take (with time)
  • Discussion and instant messaging with assigned team members
  • A talk now button to talk to a health professional or for immediate correspondence in case of depression crisis

Final Prototype - App

  • A storytelling section for users to share their stories and also, view others stories. Stories are very impactful because when individuals are exposed to others modeling a behavior that leads to a desired outcome, they are both more motivated to engage in that behavior and capable of acting in accordance with it
  • A doc blog section where associated doctors post articles about medication, fighting depression, and healthcare in general
  • In-app inbox to talk to contact doctors and other users from stories page
