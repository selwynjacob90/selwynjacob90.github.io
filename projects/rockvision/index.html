rockvision

rockvision is a new way of learning to play the guitar through augmented vision. It uses Google cardboard, phone’s rear camera, and overlayed musical content to provide a highly interactive, interruption-free, intuitive, and an immersive learning experience. This project is currently under progress.

Accolade: Our team won first place in PitchAustin Competition at SXSW 2015.

The problem

From our past experience of teaching and playing music, we observed that there was a big disconnect when learning from books, DVDs, online resources, and apps. Some of the key issues with these traditional methods include,



  • Lack of interactivity which makes it difficult to map notes with the actual musical piece / song
  • Having to look back and forth between notes and the instrument which results in loss of focus, and an increased cognitive load apart from learning the music piece itself
  • Interruptions in play to turn papers, scroll pages, or seek media content
  • Trying to keep in sync with the audio when learning, and replaying parts when out of sync
  • Overwhelming to learn lengthy, and complex pieces
  • Hinders learners from experiencing the true joy of playing music

How rockvision works

rockvision is shipped as a mobile application. Upon installation, users can open the app, and slide their phone into Google cardboard or any similar DIY case. Rockvision uses phone’s rear camera to bring back sight, and overlays content on top of it. Navigation in the application is done through voice commands, and commands corresponding to a particular context, are always persistent on the screen.

Modes

Rockvision divides the learning content into three modes. Training mode for learning chords, scales, exercises. Song mode for playing through popular songs. Finally, a solo mode for learning solos, and advanced guitar licks. We designed a staggered learning approach for each of these modes where, lengthy or complex pieces/songs are broken down into parts, and the entire piece can be played along upon completing all the parts. This not only helps in remembering a song/piece as individual smaller chunks but also prevents the learning from being overwhelmed at lengthy or complex pieces.
